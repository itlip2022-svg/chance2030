CHANCE2030

Construction site of a possible place

“May a question grow?”

Paula's Garden becomes a social construction site

Chance2030 is a social sculpture in Paula's Garden. A historic place becomes a construction site, a resonance space and a question: what could Worpswede be in the year 2030, if it no longer merely administers its cultural heritage but turns it back into presence?

The project gathers images, conversations, silence, signatures, resistances and possibilities. Not as a finished answer, but as an open process.

Perhaps the future begins where a place is no longer only shown, but once again risks something.

01Project

What is Chance2030?

Chance2030 is an ongoing artistic project between photography, construction site, conversation and social sculpture. It asks what a historic artists' place can become when memory is no longer enough.

Paula's Garden is not a finished exhibition format, but a real place in the making: garden, construction site, threshold, image space and public resonance test.

02The Place

Paula's Garden

A historic place in Worpswede becomes the construction site of a possible place. Not as a museum, not as a backdrop, but as a space in which questions, images and conversations can emerge.

03In public space

Fence conversations

At the construction fence the project begins in public space. Whoever stops, reads, rings the bell or stays silent becomes part of the process. Chance2030 gathers not only answers, but also hesitation, absence and non-resonance.

Notice at the fence
For first fence conversations, please ring the bell.

05Marker

2030Not a promise. An open marker.

2030 is not a promise. It is an open marker. A question to the place, to art, to the present: what could grow if a historic artists' place were no longer only shown, but risked once again?
